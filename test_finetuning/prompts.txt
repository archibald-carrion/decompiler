The Roman Empire was one of the largest empires in ancient history.
Julius Caesar crossed the Rubicon in 49 BCE.
Roman legions were the backbone of the empire's military might.
The Colosseum in Rome could hold up to 80,000 spectators.
Roman engineering marvels included aqueducts and roads.
The fall of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE marked the end of ancient Rome's dominance.
Marcus Aurelius, the philosopher emperor, ruled from 161 to 180 CE.
The Roman Senate served as an advisory body to the consuls and later to the emperors.
Gladiators were trained fighters who battled in amphitheaters throughout the Roman Empire.
Roman law, codified in the Twelve Tables, became the foundation for legal systems throughout Europe.
